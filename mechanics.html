In-Game Features and Mechanics

Sparking Blast

A popular and useful option that can be made for aggressive and defensive uses alike. For starters, Sparking Blast can essentially be explained like some tools in other games like 'X-Factor' in Marvel vs. Capcom 3. It will allow your character(s) to passively heal their 'blue health' while in combat, allow you to be plus on block when your super dash is guarded, jump cancel out of some moves, and more.

Things to Note:
While the game will have you start with a single charge of sparking blast, it is possible to get a second one with use of the Dragon Balls in the middle of the game.

Red and Blue Combos

These, sometimes referred to as 'combo states,' are ways to gauge if a combo is good and reliable to use or not. Because the nature of a fighting game is to balance it out and make as fair and unexploitable as possible, there is a concept of hit stun and decay. As you hit someone more in a single combo, they lose hitstun faster and the combo can be open to 'drop' making the combo and number turn blue.

Things to Note:
If a combo turns blue, that means that anyone could potentially escape said combo, so long as they're doing the right things. So a good practice would be to learn and consistently do combos that are red.
